# map.ofww1.org — Impeccable design context

## Design Context

### Users
General-public, history-curious audience. People who want to *understand* the geography of the First World War as a slowly-unfolding 4-year event, not run a tactical-history query. WWI centenary visitors, students, descendants tracing a great-grandfather's squadron, casual map-wanderers who arrive from OHM. Device mix skews desktop / large screens for the slider-scrubbing main experience, but mobile must remain a first-class adapted view.

### Brand Personality
Three words: **Sober · Tender · Documentary**. Sibling tone to openartmap and openculturemap, but a register lower and less ornamented — this is a war atlas, not a wunderkammer. The interface should feel like a printed centenary monograph: legible, restrained, image-led where images exist, generous in margin. Emotional goal: *quiet attention to the dead and the documented*, not spectacle, not data-dashboard authority.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Inspirations**: the IWM and CWGC centenary microsites at their best (sepia restraint, generous typography, the named-dead as the focus); 1914–1918-online encyclopedia; printed centenary atlases (Penguin, Osprey at their non-pulp end); poppy-field photography in the *Guardian Long Read* register.
- **Anti-references**: military-history forum aesthetics, video-game battle maps, the "history channel" colour-overdrive look, any cartoon-soldier iconography. No camouflage textures, no helmet silhouettes, no overlaid CRT scanlines. Wikipedia infobox grey-on-grey lists are also out.
- **Visual register**: a centenary monograph reimagined for the web. Warm sepia paper feel in light mode; deep ink with a single banked-ember warmth in dark. Earth-toned but sombre palette — poppy-red used **sparingly** as the single warm accent, sepia/ochre for paper, ink for body, moss for memorials.
- **Theme**: both light and dark, with a visible toggle. Default to **light** (sepia paper — matches the printed-monograph register). Dark mode is the late-evening reading variant; embers, not neon.

### Design Principles
1. **The map and the slider are the hero — the chrome is the caption.** Same as openartmap. Sidebar feels like the margin notes of a printed centenary volume.
2. **Time has weight.** The day-of-war readout is large and characterful. Scrubbing should feel like turning the pages of a chronology — and the slider passes through Verdun, the Somme, Caporetto, the armistice as visible *moments* the user can scrub onto.
3. **Characterful typography over neutral system type.** Same Caprasimo display / Vollkorn body lineage as openartmap; the family resemblance is intentional — these are sibling sites in the OHM atlas family.
4. **Earthy-sombre, not bright-loud.** Poppy-red is the single warm accent, used for the active slider thumb and the year readout only. Ochre and sepia carry the paper. Moss carries memorials. **Never** use red as an outline or fill on attack markers — the attack glyph is a falling-bomb mark in ink, not red. Red is reserved for the most human thing on the page (the slider thumb, where the user's hand sits).
5. **Decorative details should look hand-set, not Figma-stock.** Glyphs are small woodcut-ish marks — a poppy for memorial, a crossed-rifles bracket for battle, a falling-bomb tick for attack. Avoid generic rounded-square icons.
6. **Generosity of space.** Same Cabinet-magazine spacing as openartmap.
7. **Bans:** no border-left accent stripes, no gradient text, no glassmorphism, no cyan/purple/blue-to-purple gradients, no glowing accents on dark, no monospace as "tech-y" shorthand, no progress bars that look like loaders, no helmet/camouflage/rifle illustration beyond the small woodcut glyph, no animated bomb-falls or explosion effects.

### Typography Commitments
- **Display**: *Caprasimo* (same as openartmap — sibling site visual continuity).
- **Body**: *Vollkorn* (same as openartmap).
- **Day-of-war readout**: the display serif, sized large enough to be the second-loudest thing on the page after the map. Tabular-nums OpenType feature so the date doesn't jitter while scrubbing.
- **No monospace** unless we ever surface code-like content.

### Color Commitments
- Use OKLCH everywhere.
- Light theme: warm sepia paper background (~96% L, ~0.018 chroma toward 80° / warm yellow — slightly more chroma than openartmap to feel "aged paper" not "fresh paper"), ink-warm text (~22% L), poppy-red accent (~52% L / 0.18 C / ~28° hue) used *sparingly*, ochre secondary (~62% L / 0.12 C / ~65°), moss for memorials (~52% L / 0.105 C / ~142°), ink-blue for battles (~38% L / 0.08 C / ~250°).
- Dark theme: deep warm ink background (~14% L / 0.014 C / 60°), aged-paper text, the same accents tuned upward in lightness and downward in chroma to read correctly on dark.
- Neutrals are tinted toward the sepia hue, not pure gray.
- Three-kind palette: **attack = ink**, **battle = ink-blue**, **memorial = moss**. Poppy-red is reserved for the slider thumb + active day readout — the only place the user's attention is asked to land hot.
